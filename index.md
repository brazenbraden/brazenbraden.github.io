## About Me

I’m a Ruby on Rails consultant with over a decade of experience helping teams modernise legacy applications without disrupting day-to-day delivery. My work has focused on untangling brittle codebases, expanding test coverage, improving delivery workflows, and leading safe, incremental upgrades across ageing systems.

Over the years, I’ve worked on long-lived production platforms, internal business systems, and founder-led product development. I do my best work in environments where software has become hard to change, risky to release, or difficult for teams to work in confidently.

I currently operate through [**Red Oryx**](https://redoryx.co.uk){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}, my consultancy focused on legacy Ruby and Ruby on Rails modernisation, refactoring, and delivery improvement.

### Kallidus (Sapling Team)

**Position:** Senior Backend Ruby Engineer

**Duration:** Jul 2023 – May 2025

**Revamped and optimised the CircleCI pipeline for our test suite, reducing build times from ~50 minutes to under 15 minutes**

Restructured the pipeline from a single heavily parallelised job into multiple discrete jobs grouped by test type, making failures quicker to isolate and rerun. Introduced fast-running code quality checks early in the pipeline and implemented test coverage enforcement to prevent untested code from merging. These changes reduced CI credit usage, improved feedback loops, and raised engineering quality across the team.

**Refactored a legacy reporting system into a clean, extensible framework using the Builder pattern**

Inherited complex, brittle reporting code and incrementally transformed it into a robust, testable architecture. The new design standardised report development, improved maintainability, and enabled faster delivery of new reports with cleaner handoff to other engineers.

**Led the transition to a trunk-based development workflow, streamlining releases and improving collaboration**

Moved the team from a slow, single-branch QA process to weekly releases on the main branch, reducing QA overhead and improving delivery reliability. Established branch and commit conventions, integrated release tooling with GitHub, and documented the new process to support consistent adoption.
