I recently went public with my first ever game prototype, "When The Mud Came", a passion project I have been working on for the last few months. It’s rough. It’s unfinished. That's what a prototype is after all. Most importantly, it exists, it’s fully playable and already a realisation of the kind of game I want to play.

Where to next? What's the plan? This post aims to answer those questions and give you an idea of the intended roadmap ahead.

![Cleaning some muck]({{site.images}}/whenthemudcame/roadmap/cleaning.png)

---

## What the current demo actually is

The current demo is not a “vertical slice”, and it’s definitely not content-complete.

What it *is* is a test of the core loop and supporting systems:
- Satisfying muck cleaning
- Seeing colour come back to the world in real time
- The dopamine hit every time you unlock a new upgrade

That’s the heart of the game. Everything else hangs off that.

Play the demo. You'll see the rough edges, the AI placeholder art, the inconsistency in cleaning of some objects. This is expected right now but the question that must be answered is:

> Is this fun? Is this something I want to keep playing?

Personally, I say yes. I hope you do too.

---

## What I’m focusing on right now

At the moment, the work is very much about **tightening**, not expanding.

That mostly means spending time on things that aren’t especially flashy, but make a big difference once they’re done:

- Improving how dirt, grime, and wet surfaces behave visually while cleaning
- Refining the existing demo area so it feels more cohesive and deliberate
- Expanding progression and upgrades
- Improving UI clarity and general usability
- A constant background pass of polish across visuals, audio, and overall feel

It won’t be ready for a Steam release after this — but it should move the project from ‘prototype’ to something more concrete and intentional.

---

## Looking beyond the demo

There *is* a clear idea of where the game could go beyond the current demo — but I want to be careful with how that’s framed.

These are **directions**, not promises.

Broadly speaking, that future involves things like:
- Additional locations beyond the current demo area
- More depth in progression and upgrades
- New mechanics that build on the existing cleaning loop
- Further development of the game’s atmosphere and tone
- A more cohesive story, with optional side content

Exactly how, when, or even *if* all of that lands depends on a lot of factors — feedback, time, energy, and the realities of being one human with a keyboard.

I’d much rather keep this honest and flexible than start drawing lines in the sand I’ll regret later.

## Following along

*When The Mud Came* is a solo project and very much a passion thing — it’s a game I want to play myself, first and foremost.

Progress tends to happen in small, deliberate steps rather than big dramatic leaps. There will be periods of visible change, and others where the work is mostly invisible but still important.

If you want to follow along:
- I’ll post updates as the demo evolves
- Bigger shifts will be reflected on the public roadmap
- Longer write-ups and devlogs will appear here from time to time

There’s no set timeline; there are many factors at play, and time is limited — so progress will be steady rather than rushed.

If you’ve already played the demo, shared feedback, or even just said something encouraging — genuinely, thank you. It helps more than you might think.
